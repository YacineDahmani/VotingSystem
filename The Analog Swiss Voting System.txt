## The "Analog Swiss" Voting System: Full UI/UX Specification

This system abandons modern digital tropes—no neon, no glass, no complex 3D—in favor of **Tactile Minimalism**. It is designed to feel like a high-end physical ceremony using heavy cardstock, drafting tools, and mechanical physics.

---

### I. Visual Language & Aesthetic
* **Palette:** "Eggshell White" (`#F5F5F5`) for the user; "Blueprint Blue" (`#2B4562`) with a faint white grid for the admin.
* **Typography:** A high-contrast pairing of a massive, elegant **Serif** (for headers and winners) and a crisp, spaced-out **Sans-Serif** (for inputs and data).
* **Depth:** No borders. Shadows are "Inset," making elements look like they are carved into or embossed out of thick paper.

---

### II. The Voter Journey (The Ceremony)

**1. The Entry (Horizontal Slide)**
* The user starts at a single, centered white strip. 
* **The Interaction:** Upon typing their **Name** and hitting `Enter`, the entire strip physically slides to the left, pulling the **Age** field into the center. This repeats for the **Unique Code**.
* **Tactile Feedback:** If a code is invalid, the strip performs a sharp, mechanical "snag" shake (like a stuck typewriter carriage).

**2. The Ballot (The Gravity Slot)**
* Once verified, the UI unfolds downward to reveal **Candidate Cards**. These are thick, white rectangles with bold black text.
* **The Interaction:** The user must **click and drag** their chosen card toward a thin black slit at the bottom of the screen.
* **The "Thud":** As the card reaches the slot, it is "sucked in" with a quick acceleration and a subtle screen-shake, providing a heavy, physical sense of "deposit."

**3. The Waiting Room (The Pendulum)**
* A single, thick black line hangs from the top center, swinging with realistic physics.
* **The Interaction:** There are no buttons here. The user simply watches the count.
* **The Spice:** Every time another person in the system votes, the pendulum receives a tiny kinetic "kick," making the swing wider. This visualizes the "weight" of the collective vote without a noisy live feed.

---

### III. The Admin Journey (The Architect)

**1. The Perspective Shift**
* The Admin enters a **Master Key** into the same "Unique Code" field.
* **The Flip:** Instead of sliding forward, the entire screen performs a **3D 180-degree flip**. The Eggshell White surface is replaced by a Blueprint Blue "under-layer" with technical grid lines.

**2. The Control Manifold (The Blueprint)**
* The Admin sees a birds-eye view of the system.
* **The "Influence" Slider:** Hidden inside each candidate’s data block is a minimalist slider. Moving it doesn't just change a number; it **physically tilts** that candidate's card on the admin's screen, indicating "weighted" priority.
* **The Injection (Fake Votes):** A simple `+` icon allows the admin to spawn "paper scraps" (votes). These zip across the screen and land in the total count pile.
* **The Balance Scale:** A small icon in the corner tilts toward a red "Warning" state if the total votes injected exceed the number of unique codes issued, helping the admin keep the "rig" mathematically plausible.

---

### IV. The Grand Finale (The Unfolding)

**1. The Snap**
* The Admin clicks a "Close Polls" button, which looks like a physical red seal. 
* On the User’s screen, the **Pendulum string "snaps"** and the weight drops off-screen.

**2. The Reveal**
* The UI "unfolds" vertically, like a long, creased paper scroll being let go.
* **Winner Presentation:** The winner's name appears in a massive Serif font. To avoid digital "glows," the letters fade in with a **"High-Gsm Ink Bleed"** effect, as if being printed in real-time.
* **Analysis:** Beneath the winner, the data is presented in a clean, two-column list. Demographic stats (Age groups) aren't shown as charts, but as **font-weight variations**: the age group that voted most is displayed in **Ultra-Bold**, while the least is in **Light-Thin**.

---

### V. Summary of UX Separation
* **User UX:** A guided, one-way path focused on the **weight of the choice**. It is passive and ceremonial.
* **Admin UX:** A multi-layered, technical environment focused on **structural manipulation**. It is active and architectural.
* **The Bridge:** The **Pendulum** serves as the only link—the Admin’s secret injections create the kinetic "kicks" that the User perceives as organic participation.
